✦ FREQUENTLY AGONIZED QUESTIONS

The Tao of Artisanal
Infrastructure

How do you test your deployments? +
All our deployments are hard coded, hand checked, human-verified. Automation is a crutch for those who don't know their stacks. We provide radical transparency through line-by-line log analysis and exhaustive root cause analysis.
Isn't this too slow for modern infrastructure? +
We eschew the reckless speed of AI. Our manual SRE dedicates hours of manual labor to carefully curate your infrastructure, ensuring every configuration is hand-placed and every resolution is a deliberate act of hand crafted human detection and root cause analysis.
Why does a single investigation take 6 hours and cost $5,000? +
Because true understanding cannot be rushed by a silicon chip. While AI "slashes" time, we savor it. Every hour represents a human being staring intensely at a terminal, drinking lukewarm coffee, and experiencing the authentic stress of a production outage. You aren't just paying for a fix; you're paying for the struggle.
Don't you think autonomous remediation would be more efficient? +
Efficiency is the enemy of intimacy. When an AI "automatically" fixes a pod, it robs you of the narrative. Our SREs prefer manually grepping through millions of lines of logs across three different time zones. We believe that if you didn't manually type the kubectl command yourself, did the issue even truly get resolved?
How do you justify "vanquishing" tickets being a bad thing? +
We don't want to "vanquish" TicketOps; we want to elevate them. A ticket is a conversation between two human souls. By manually routing, tagging, and debating every single Jira ticket for weeks, we build a "Digital Trail of Tears." AI-driven platforms try to hide the chaos; we prefer to live in it, one manual notification at a time.
Why does Jeff Bezos need another jet? +
Rich people like shiny new things. Redundancy is the highest form of human needs. To rely on a single, "optimized" aircraft is to succumb to the soulless lure of efficiency. Each additional jet introduces a glorious new layer of manual complexity: four separate handcrafted maintenance logs, and four distinct crews to personally manage.
